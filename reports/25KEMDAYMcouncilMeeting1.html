The meeting was called to order at 11:05 hrs by the Youth President who acknowledged the presence of the District Pastor (Pastor Prince Hankwebe) and the District Secretary (Elder Moonga).

Pastor Hankwebe encouraged unity and emphasized free interaction and good communication to ensure the youth ministry runs smoothly. He rendered an apology for not being able to stay for the entire meeting due to prior commitments, but assured the council that the District Secretary would remain available. He offered a prayer before leaving.

The Youth President thanked all present churches and introduced the 2025 newly elected executive members from Zones A and B. Thereafter, the agenda was read and adopted by the council members.

Main Business Resolutions:

1. Invitation Letter from Shimukuni Mission District:
Kabwe East Mission District received an invitation from Shimukuni Mission District (Kalola Zone) for a camp that was to run from April 6-12, 2025 at Kalola Campsite. Registration fee is tagged at K120, with food and transport to have been organized by the district. The council agreed to respond after discussing time management.

2. Vacancies:
The following members were elected to the executive:
Zone A:
- Vice Treasurer: Br. Charles Musakanya (Silos SDA Church)
- Ambassador Leader: Sr. Grenda Chitanika (Nabusanga SDA Church)
- Planning/Organizer: Br. Justine Mulwanda (Lukanga SDA Church)
Zone B:
- Vice Secretary: Br. Gift ( SDA Church)

3. Global Youth Day:
GYD 2025 was to be celebrated at district level under the theme "Act as a Community Transformed". Council members were to purchase T-shirts of any color. The council agreed to visit an orphanage, with each church contributing to the event. Further details were to be posted on the forum.

4. Induction and Enrollment Ceremony Experience:
Observations:
- Excessive comedy in some churches
- Inappropriate drills (entertainment drill, slow match, quick match)
- Incorrect candle arrangements and shortages
- Improper salutations and permission granting
- Poor communication between local and district leaders
- Lack of coordination between Master Guides and church administration
- Limited knowledge of proper ceremony procedures
- Uniform inconsistencies
- Shortage of teaching materials
- Inconsistent forms across churches
Recommendations:
- Conduct lessons on proper ceremony procedures
- District leaders to produce and sell standardized candles
- Improved coordination between district and church leaders
- Advance planning and preparation by churches
- District to advise on correct forms
- Education department to provide teaching materials to all churches by year-end

5. Time Management:
After forum discussion (Mighty KEMD), the council agreed:
- Budget should be approximate rather than fixed
- Cancel second quarter community service
The amended time management was adopted.

6. Financial Report:
The Vice President apologized for the unavailability of the previous financial report. The District Secretary assured the matter would be addressed and encouraged support for new executives. The 2025 financial report was read and adopted.

7. Any Other Business:
- The Shimukuni invitation was accepted
- Members encouraged to promote GYD and upcoming programs

Closing Remarks: The President appreciated members' attendance and encouraged peace, love, and unity.

Closing Prayer: The meeting concluded with prayer.

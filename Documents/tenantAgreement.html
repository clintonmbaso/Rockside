SECTION B: Use of the Premises

  1. The Caretaker agrees to occupy the accommodation as his only or principal premises. He/she agrees not to use the accommodation for illegal or immoral activities, nor for any business or trade purposes. The premises should not be used for business or trade purposes without written permission from Rockside SDA Church.
  2. The Caretaker agrees to tell Rockside SDA Church if he is to be absent from the premises for any reason for a period of more than fourteen days. The Caretaker agrees to take such measures to secure the Church property prior to such absence as the Church may reasonably require and take appropriate measures to prevent loss or damage.
  3. The Caretaker agrees to take care and account for all the Church properties in liaison with the Church deacons. He/she agrees to ensure that all Church properties in the premises are properly secured and protected against any abuse, misuse and loss.
  4. The Caretaker agrees not to lend any Church properties with the prior knowledge and authority of the Church Head Deacon/ess.
  5. The Caretaker agrees to keep the Church premises in a clean and tenable environment at all times, in liaison with the Church Deacons.
  6. The Caretaker agrees Not to:
    • Assign this care to any other person; or
    • Sublet the premises in whole or in part; or
    • Take in lodgers or paying guests; or
    • Allow other persons to share the occupancy of the premises, whether or not for payment, without the prior written consent of Church.
  7. The Caretaker agrees to take reasonable care of the Church premises and any common parts, and in particular agrees to take all reasonable steps to:
    • Keep the premises cleaned, including the floor/tiles;
    • Not bring any hazardous or combustible goods or material into the premises;
    • Not to pour any oil, grease, or other damaging materials down the floor, walls or door
    • Avoid danger to the premises or neighbouring properties by way of fire or flooding.
  8. The Caretaker agrees not to make any alteration to the premises, its fixtures or fittings, nor to carry out any internal or external decoration without the prior written consent of the Church.
  9. In the case of flatted property incorporating communal areas the Caretaker undertakes, in conjunction with the deacons/ess, to sweep and clean the common areas. The Caretaker will ensure that the outside, backyard or other communal area will be kept in a clean and tidy condition.
  10. The Caretaker agrees not to alter, change or install any locks on any doors or windows in or about the Property or have any additional keys made for any locks without the prior written consent of the Church.
  11. The Caretaker agrees to dispose of all rubbish in an appropriate manner and at the appropriate time. Rubbish must not be placed anywhere in the premises, or at the water tap at any time.
  12. The Caretaker agrees Not to:
    • Cause (nor allow other occupiers, guests or visitors to the premises to cause) nuisance, annoyance or disturbance to neighbours, or to the church, her agents or employees either in the premises.
    • Commit or allow members of his/her household or persons visiting the premises to commit any form of harassment on the grounds of race, colour, religion, gender, sexual orientation, disability or age which may interfere with the peace and comfort of, or cause offence to, any other neighbours or members of their household either in their premises or in the locality of the church premises.
  13. The Caretaker agrees not to keep any animals or pets in the premises without the prior written consent of the Church. Any such consent will not be unreasonably withheld.
  14. The Caretaker agrees to:
    • Give the Church, or anyone authorised by the Church, access to the premises for the purpose of carrying out maintenance, repair, inspection or any proper purpose providing that notice has been given to the Caretaker not later than 24 hours beforehand that such access is required.
    • Give immediate access to the Church in an emergency whether or not notice has been given. The Church reserves the right to effect forcible entry to the premises should such access not be made available.

SECTION C: Undertakings

  1. The Landlord (Church) agrees throughout the period of the tenancy to maintain the accommodation in a tenable condition and in all other respects reasonably fit for human habitation.
  2. The Landlord (Church) undertakes (together with any other owners of common parts of the building in which the accommodation is situated, if appropriate) to keep in repair the structure and exterior of the accommodation including the following as defined by the Housing Act 2006:
    • The house is in all other respects reasonably fit for human habitation,
    • The structure and exterior of the house (including drains, gutters and external pipes) are in a reasonable state of repair and in proper working order,
  3. The Church will keep in repair and in proper working order the installations in the premises for the supply of water, electricity, sanitation, (with the exception of those installed by the tenant or which the tenant is entitled to remove) including the following:
    • Any fixtures, fittings and appliances provided by the Church under the Caretaker are in a reasonable state of repair and in proper working order,
    • Any furnishings provided by the Church under the tenancy are capable of being used safely for the purpose for which they are designed, and
    • The Church will repair or replace any of the fixtures, fittings or furnishings, supplied by the Church in the premises, which become defective and will do so within a reasonable period of time.
  4. The Caretaker undertakes to immediately notify the Church of any deterioration of the Property or of the fixtures, fittings, furnishings, and effects whatever may be the cause thereof. The Church undertakes to carry out necessary repairs within a reasonable period of time after having been notified of the need to do so.
  5. The Caretaker will be liable for the cost of repairs where the need for them is attributable to his fault or negligence, that of any person residing with him, or any guest of his.

SECTION D: Ending the Tenancy

The Church may terminate the tenancy by service on the Caretaker of a notice to quit. The Landlord may serve such notice either:

  • To terminate the tenancy at its end date, or
  • To terminate the tenancy where the tenant has materially breached any of his obligations under this Agreement. In the event that the tenant fails to remove from the accommodation at the end of the period specified in such a notice to quit, the Church may bring legal action against the Caretaker to recover possession of the premises on one or more of the grounds set out in the previous sections.
